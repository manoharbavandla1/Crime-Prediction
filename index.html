About the Application

Crime rate prediction plays a crucial role in enhancing law enforcement strategies by helping agencies identify and understand patterns of crime. By anticipating where crime is likely to occur, law enforcement can more effectively allocate resources to areas at higher risk, potentially reducing crime rates and increasing public safety. Additionally, predictive models help police departments create more effective crime response strategies.

The dataset used for this project is manually curated based on publications from the Indian National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), covering crime statistics in 19 major Indian metropolitan cities from 2014 to 2021. This application predicts crime rates for 10 distinct crime categories across these cities in the coming years. The categories include murder, kidnapping, crimes against women, crimes against children, juvenile delinquency, crimes against senior citizens, crimes against scheduled castes (SC), crimes against scheduled tribes (ST), economic offenses, and cyber crimes.

The project employs scikit-learn's Random Forest Regression model, which predicts crime rates using input features such as the year, city, and crime type. Random Forest Regression is an ensemble learning technique that builds multiple decision trees and combines their predictions, providing more accurate results than a single tree. The model achieves a prediction accuracy of 93.20% on the testing dataset, demonstrating its potential for supporting data-driven decision-making in crime prevention.
